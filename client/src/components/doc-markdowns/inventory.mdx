# Inventory Management

The Inventory module in EaseInv allows you to catalog products, track stock counts in real time, configure selling units, and analyze profit margins with granular price breakdowns.

---

## Adding Products to Inventory

To catalog a new product:
1. Navigate to the **Inventory** section in the store dashboard.
2. Click the **Add Product** button in the upper right.
3. Fill out the core catalog fields:
   * **Name**: The display name of the product.
   * **SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)**: A unique identifier for tracking inventory.
   * **GTIN (Global Trade Item Number)**: Barcode/EAN number (optional).
   * **Description**: Item specifications or remarks (optional).
   * **Buying Price**: The cost price per item at which you purchase stock. Used to calculate profit margins.

---

## Stock Tracking

EaseInv supports tracking physical stock counts to prevent overselling. 

### Enabling Stock Tracking
1. **Per-Product**: In the Product Form, toggle **Enable Inventory Tracking**. When enabled, you can enter the **Initial/Total Stock** value.
2. **Globally**: You can control stock tracking defaults in **Settings > Inventory Settings** under the **Enable Stock Tracking** switch.

> [!NOTE]
> If stock tracking is disabled for a product, it can be added to invoices infinitely without enforcing limits or issuing warnings.

## Product Units & Packaging

Every product has a primary unit (e.g. `PCS`, `BOX`, `KG`, `LTR`).
To add new units:
1. Navigate to your store **Settings > Inventory Settings**.
2. Under the **Add Custom Units** section, type your custom unit name (e.g., `BOX`, `PKT`) and click **Add**.
3. Once saved, these custom units become available in the **Stock Unit** selection dropdown when creating or updating any product.

## How Price Breakdown Works

EaseInv features a powerful **Price Breakdown Input** to support custom, quantity-based bulk pricing. This allows you to sell the same product at different price rates depending on the bulk amount.

For example, a soda can might be sold for:
* `$2` per `1 PCS`
* `$20` per `12 PCS` (case)

### Profit Margin Calculations
For each price breakdown row, the system calculates and displays the margin percentage automatically:

1. **Cost Per Item**: The unit selling price is computed as:
   `Cost Per Item = Selling Price / Breakdown Quantity`
2. **Profit Margin**: The profit margin percentage is calculated relative to your **Buying Price**:
   `Profit Margin (%) = ((Cost Per Item - Buying Price Per Item) / Buying Price Per Item) * 100`

The margin indicator is dynamically updated in the UI:
* **Green**: Positive margin (Profit).
* **Red**: Negative margin (Loss).
